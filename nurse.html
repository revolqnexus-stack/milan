📄 Full Exam Version — Write in Answer Sheet

📝 Introduction

The heart is a muscular pumping organ of the cardiovascular system. It is located in the mediastinum (space between the two lungs) in the thoracic cavity. Its main function is to pump blood to all parts of the body.

📐 Diagram — Draw this (2 marks guaranteed)

Superior Vena Cava ↓ ┌──────────────────────┐ │ RIGHT ║ LEFT │ │ ATRIUM ║ ATRIUM │← Pulmonary Veins │ ↓ ║ ↓ │ │ TRICUSP ║ MITRAL │ │ VALVE ║ VALVE │ │ ↓ ║ ↓ │ │ RIGHT ║ LEFT │ │VENTRICLE ║VENTRICLE │ └──────────────────────┘ ↓ ↓ Pulmonary Aorta Artery (to body) Labels: RA, LA, RV, LV, Aorta, Pulmonary artery, Tricuspid valve, Mitral valve, SVC, IVC

🔹 Location

  • In the mediastinum, between lungs
  • Behind the sternum (breastbone)
  • 2/3rd portion is on the left side
  • Size: like a closed fist (~300 grams)

🔹 Layers of Heart Wall

  • Pericardium — outer covering (2 layers)
  • Epicardium — thin outer layer
  • Myocardium — middle muscular layer (thickest)
  • Endocardium — smooth inner layer

🔹 Chambers

  • 4 chambers: Right Atrium, Left Atrium, Right Ventricle, Left Ventricle
  • Atria = upper chambers (receive blood)
  • Ventricles = lower chambers (pump blood)
  • Septum separates right and left sides

🔹 Valves

  • Tricuspid valve — between RA and RV (3 flaps)
  • Bicuspid/Mitral valve — between LA and LV (2 flaps)
  • Pulmonary semilunar valve — RV to pulmonary artery
  • Aortic semilunar valve — LV to aorta

🔹 Blood Supply (Coronary circulation)

  • Right coronary artery and Left coronary artery
  • They arise from the aorta

🔹 Conducting System (Pacemaker)

  • SA Node — Sino-Atrial Node = Pacemaker of heart (starts heartbeat)
  • AV Node — Atrio-Ventricular Node (delays impulse)
  • Bundle of His — carries impulse to ventricles
  • Purkinje fibres — spread impulse to ventricle walls

🩺 Nursing Importance

Nurses monitor heart rate, BP and ECG. Knowledge of heart anatomy helps understand heart attacks (MI), CCF, and cardiac emergencies.

✅ Conclusion

The heart plays a vital role in circulating blood throughout the body. Understanding its structure helps nurses provide better cardiac care.

📝 Introduction

The kidney is the main excretory organ of the body, part of the urinary system. There are 2 kidneys, located in the retroperitoneal space (behind the abdomen). Each kidney weighs about 150 grams.

📐 Diagram

┌──────────────────────┐ │ CORTEX │ ← outer layer │ ┌────────────┐ │ │ │ MEDULLA │ │ ← inner layer │ │ (Pyramids) │ │ │ └────┬───────┘ │ │ │ PELVIS │ └───────┼──────────────┘ ↓ URETER Nephron labels: Bowman's capsule, Glomerulus, PCT, Loop of Henle, DCT, Collecting duct

🔹 Location

  • Behind the peritoneum (retroperitoneal)
  • T12 to L3 vertebral level
  • Right kidney slightly lower than left (due to liver)

🔹 Structure

  • Cortex — outer region, contains nephrons
  • Medulla — inner region, contains renal pyramids
  • Renal pelvis — funnel-shaped, collects urine
  • Ureter — takes urine to bladder

🔹 Nephron (Functional unit)

  • Each kidney has 1 million nephrons
  • Glomerulus — ball of capillaries (filtration)
  • Bowman's capsule — surrounds glomerulus
  • PCT — proximal convoluted tubule (reabsorption)
  • Loop of Henle — concentration of urine
  • DCT — distal convoluted tubule (secretion)
  • Collecting duct — final concentration

🔹 Functions of Kidney

  • Filters blood → forms urine
  • Maintains water balance
  • Regulates blood pressure (renin)
  • Produces erythropoietin (RBC production)
  • Maintains pH of blood

📝 Introduction

A neuron is the functional unit of the nervous system. It transmits nerve impulses (signals) from one part of the body to another. The body has about 100 billion neurons.

📐 Diagram

Dendrites → Cell Body (Nucleus) ↑↑↑ │ (receive) │ Axon hillock │ ════╪════ ← Myelin sheath │ (Nodes of Ranvier) ════╪════ │ Axon terminals (release) Labels: Dendrites, Cell body, Nucleus, Axon, Myelin sheath, Schwann cell, Node of Ranvier, Axon terminal, Synapse

🔹 Parts of Neuron

  • Dendrites — short branches, receive impulses
  • Cell body (soma) — contains nucleus, processes signals
  • Axon — long fibre, carries impulse away from cell body
  • Myelin sheath — fatty covering, speeds up conduction
  • Node of Ranvier — gaps in myelin sheath
  • Axon terminal — releases neurotransmitters
  • Synapse — junction between two neurons

🔹 Types of Neurons

  • Sensory neurons — carry signals TO brain
  • Motor neurons — carry signals FROM brain to muscles
  • Interneurons — connect sensory and motor neurons

🔹 Types of Joints

📝 Definition

Urine formation is the process by which the kidneys filter blood and produce urine to remove waste products from the body.

🔹 3 Steps of Urine Formation

  1. Glomerular Filtration
    • Blood is filtered in the glomerulus
    • Water, glucose, salts, urea pass into Bowman's capsule
    • This is called filtrate
    • Rate = 125 ml/min (GFR)
  2. Tubular Reabsorption
    • Useful substances are taken back into blood
    • Glucose, amino acids, water, salts are reabsorbed
    • Happens in PCT, Loop of Henle, DCT
  3. Tubular Secretion
    • Extra waste is pushed from blood into tubule
    • H+ ions, K+ ions, creatinine are secreted

🔹 Normal Urine Facts

  • Amount: 1000–1500 ml/day
  • pH: 4.6–8.0 (normally slightly acidic)
  • Colour: pale yellow (due to urochrome)
  • Specific gravity: 1.002–1.035

Infection is the invasion and multiplication of microorganisms in the body that causes disease.

✅ How Examiners Award Marks

What Examiners LOVE

  • Neat labelled diagram drawn first — even partial theory gets 2 marks
  • Bullet points with headings — not paragraphs
  • Underlined keywords — SA Node, Nephron, Autoclave, Filtration
  • Numbered steps for processes (urine formation, cardiac cycle, Gram stain)
  • Definition in first line — "[Topic] is defined as..."
  • Nursing importance line — shows clinical application
  • Full pages — short empty answers signal incomplete knowledge

Marking Psychology

  • Examiners scan for headings + diagram + keywords — not perfect English
  • 4-mark short note: 1 def + 3 bullets + tiny diagram = full marks
  • 7-mark: list (3) + diagram (4) OR explain (4) + diagram (3)
  • 8-mark: organs list + structure + diagram + functions + nursing
  • Wrong but relevant points get partial marks — never leave blank
